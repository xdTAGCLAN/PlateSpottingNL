License Plates of
The Netherlands (NL)

Photographed in The Netherlands

Sidecode 10. Military Series. D-*-M = Military.

Sidecode 3. Military Series. KZ = Koninklijke Landmacht (Army). Army plates are the only (current) plate types with no euroband.

Sidecode 1. Military Series. LM = Koninklijke Luchtmacht (Air Force). Army plates are the only (current) plate types with no euroband.

Sidecode 1. Diplomatic Series. CD = Corps Diplomatique. No Country Coding.

Sidecode 1. Diplomatic Series. CD = Corps Diplomatique. No Country Coding. US-Sized plate.

Sidecode 3. Diplomatic Series. CD = Corps Diplomatique. No Country Coding. CD inbetween the numerals are for International Organizations.

Sidecode 1. Diplomatic Series. BN = Temporary / foreigner. 90-00 - 99-99 = Private vehicles of International Organizations, U.S. and Belgian military personnel attached to NATO.

Sidecode 2. Diplomatic Series. BN = Temporary / foreigner. 90-00 - 99-99 = Private vehicles of International Organizations, U.S. and Belgian military personnel attached to NATO.

Sidecode 1. Diplomatic Series. Diplomatic series. GN = Geen Nederlander (Not a dutchman). 70-00 - 88-99 = Motor vehicles and trailers of embassy and consulate staff.

Sidecode 2. Diplomatic Series. Diplomatic series. GN = Geen Nederlander (Not a dutchman). 90-00 - 99-99 = Tax-exempt motor vehicles.

Dutch Forces in Germany. N = Netherlands Forces in Germany.

No Sidecode. Royal House Series. AA = Dutch Royal Family.

No Sidecode. Royal House Series. AA = Dutch Royal Family. White on Black = Oldtimer.
